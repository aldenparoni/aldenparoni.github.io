Back in my senior year of high school, I took ceramics as an elective just for fun. I went into it thinking that it was chill, but I didn't expect putting so much time and effort in the studio. Being the detail-oriented person that I am, I had the studio all to myself after school one day just so I could finish one of my projects. Admittedly, I'm extremely proud of all the pieces I made in that class, especially my three-part piece pictured here. Each part of the process was difficult, from molding the clay, to using tools to carve out fine details, to adding the glaze, and then just waiting for your piece to come out of the kiln hopefully in one piece. However, seeing your piece emerge shiny and in color is a rewarding experience, making it all worth it.
